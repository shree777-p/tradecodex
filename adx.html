ADX

ADX (Average Directional Index) measures how strong a trend is, without indicating whether that trend is up or down.

What ADX measures

ADX is plotted on a 0–100 scale. Low readings (commonly below 20–25) suggest a weak or absent trend — often a sideways, range-bound market. Higher readings suggest a strong trend, though ADX says nothing about which direction that trend is moving in.

Reading ADX levels

A rising ADX suggests trend strength is increasing, regardless of direction; a falling ADX suggests the current trend (whatever it is) is losing steam. Because of this, ADX is often used to decide whether trend-following tools are likely to work well right now, rather than as a buy or sell signal itself.

Combining with +DI and -DI

ADX is typically plotted alongside two companion lines, +DI and -DI, which do indicate direction — the relationship between them shows whether buying or selling pressure currently dominates, while ADX itself shows how strongly that pressure is being expressed.
